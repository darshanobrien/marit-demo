# Personas

## Business User

Business Users are employees or teams with operational pain points. They may not know which AI tools are available or whether AI is the right solution.

### Goals

- Describe a business problem in plain language.
- Understand whether AI might help.
- See what information the AI Lab needs.
- Track whether their request is being reviewed.

### Needs

- Simple intake language.
- Examples and prompts that clarify what to submit.
- Transparent status.
- Confidence that confidential details will be handled appropriately in production.

### Frictions

- May overestimate or underestimate what AI can do.
- May omit data availability, process constraints, or regulatory context.
- May use business-specific terminology.

## AI Builder

AI Builders are members of the internal AI Lab who prototype, evaluate, and help productionize AI solutions.

### Goals

- Quickly triage opportunities.
- Identify tool fit and feasibility.
- Understand responsible AI risk early.
- Decide next steps for discovery or prototyping.

### Needs

- Consistent assessment format.
- Clear scoring rationale.
- List of relevant tools and why they fit.
- Review workflow that separates AI-generated suggestions from human judgment.

### Frictions

- Poorly scoped requests.
- Missing data and process details.
- Risky ideas that need careful handling.
- Too many intake requests for available builder capacity.

## Admin

Admins represent configuration ownership for the AI tools catalogue. Administrators maintain an inventory of approved, restricted, pilot, and deprecated AI tools, as well as AI tools currently under review. Business cases are assessed not just abstractly, but against the actual AI capabilities available to the firm.

### Goals

- Support demo setup and reset if needed.
- Manage available, restricted, pilot, deprecated, and under-review AI tools.
- Eventually manage responsible AI pillar definitions.

### Needs

- Clear future configuration boundaries.
- Role-aware access expectations.

### Frictions

- Admin access is simulated for the first demo, so production authorization is still future work.
