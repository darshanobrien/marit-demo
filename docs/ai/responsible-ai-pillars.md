# Responsible AI Pillars

Marit uses the product owner's locked 10-pillar demo set for early opportunity assessment. These are demo-friendly categories intended to guide AI Builder review, not replace policy review.

## Scoring

Each pillar uses a risk score from 0 to 100:

- 0 to 24: Low risk.
- 25 to 49: Moderate risk.
- 50 to 74: High risk.
- 75 to 100: Critical risk.

Scores should be accompanied by explanation, concerns, pros, cons, and mitigation ideas. For the first demo, concise bullet content is enough.

## Pillars

## 1. Human Oversight

Assesses whether people remain meaningfully involved in review, decisions, escalation, and accountability.

## 2. Fairness and Non-Discrimination

Assesses whether the solution could create or amplify unfair outcomes for people, teams, customers, or communities.

## 3. Privacy and Data Protection

Assesses personal data, confidential business data, retention, access, and consent implications.

## 4. Security and Robustness

Assesses risks related to unauthorized access, prompt injection, data leakage, secrets, system misuse, resilience, and adversarial behaviour.

## 5. Transparency and Explainability

Assesses whether users can understand what the AI is doing, what it used, how much to trust it, and when content is AI-generated.

## 6. Accountability and Governance

Assesses ownership, review responsibilities, governance fit, and whether decisions can be traced to accountable humans.

## 7. Legal and Regulatory Compliance

Assesses whether the idea may trigger legal, regulatory, contractual, records, accessibility, or internal policy requirements.

## 8. Data Quality and Provenance

Assesses whether appropriate data appears available, usable, representative, traceable, and maintainable.

## 9. Reliability and Performance

Assesses accuracy expectations, failure modes, evaluation approach, performance needs, and operational resilience.

## 10. Social, Environmental, and Reputational Impact

Assesses broader stakeholder impact, environmental cost, reputational risk, organizational change, and public or employee trust implications.

## Assessment Output Per Pillar

Each pillar assessment should include:

- Risk score.
- Explanation.
- Concerns.
- Pros.
- Cons.
- Mitigation ideas where appropriate.
