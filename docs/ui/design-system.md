# Design System

## Design Direction

Marit should feel like an internal enterprise tool: calm, clear, structured, and efficient. Avoid a marketing-style landing page for the app experience. The first screen should help users submit, review, or triage business cases.

## Core UI Qualities

- Dense enough for operational work.
- Easy to scan.
- Strong hierarchy without visual clutter.
- Accessible status and risk indicators.
- Clear distinction between AI-generated content and AI Builder review.

## Components Needed Early

- App shell with role switcher.
- Locale switcher.
- Navigation.
- Business case intake form.
- Business case cards or table rows.
- Score indicators with text labels.
- Risk and status badges using colour plus text/icon.
- Assessment report sections.
- Responsible AI pillar score list.
- Review notes form.
- Empty, loading, validation, and error states.

## Colour and Status

Do not rely on colour alone. Always pair colour with text, icon, or shape.

Suggested semantic states:

- Low risk.
- Moderate risk.
- High risk.
- Critical risk.
- Draft.
- Submitted.
- Assessed.
- Reviewed.

## Typography

Use readable system fonts or a lightweight default. Avoid display-heavy styling that makes the app feel like a landing page.

## Layout

- Use a persistent shell for navigation, role, and language.
- Use tables or compact lists for prioritization.
- Use sectioned reports for assessment details.
- Keep forms readable and grouped by topic.

## Accessibility Defaults

- Visible focus indicators.
- Semantic landmarks.
- Proper labels and descriptions.
- Error messages connected to fields.
- Keyboard-accessible menus and tabs.
- Accessible names for icon buttons.
